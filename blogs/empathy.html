Empathy: The Superpower Hiding in Plain Sight

June 10, 2024 5 min read Leadership & Growth

A while ago, I was leading a team that was under intense pressure to deliver on a project. One of my team members, usually full of energy, started missing deadlines. The old me might have jumped straight to reprimanding him, but something told me to pause.

Instead of asking, “Why haven’t you finished this?” I asked, “Are you okay?”

He broke down. His mother was ill, and he had been spending nights at the hospital. In that moment, the deadline wasn’t the issue—he was carrying a personal storm. Because I chose empathy over frustration, we adjusted timelines, and the rest of the team stepped in to support him. Not only did we complete the project, but the bond within the team grew stronger.

That’s when I realized: empathy doesn’t just make you a better leader—it makes you human.

Empathy and Resilience: My Personal Wake-Up Call

Not long after, I faced my own test. I went through a season where life felt overwhelming—career demands, family responsibilities, and personal setbacks all collided at once. I wore my “strong face” in public, but inside, I was drained.

What kept me from crumbling wasn’t advice or solutions—it was a friend who noticed. She didn’t lecture me. She didn’t tell me to “cheer up.” She simply said, “I can see this is heavy. You don’t have to carry it alone.”

That simple act of empathy gave me the strength to breathe, regroup, and keep moving. It taught me that empathy isn’t just something we give others—it’s a lifeline we sometimes need ourselves.

Why Empathy Feels Like a Superpower

Empathy doesn’t solve problems instantly, but it creates the space where solutions become possible. It:

  • Builds trust faster than authority ever can.
  • Strengthens teams, because people perform better when they feel understood.
  • Fuels resilience, by reminding us that we don’t have to face challenges alone.

In leadership, empathy can turn a struggling team into a thriving one. In personal life, it can turn a breaking point into a turning point.

How to Grow Your Empathy Muscle

  • Pause before reacting. Emotions often have roots deeper than what we see.
  • Ask open questions. “What’s on your mind?” or “How are you really feeling?”
  • Be present. Put down your phone, look into their eyes, and listen.
  • Offer compassion, not solutions. Sometimes, people just need you to sit with them.
Final Thought
Empathy is not weakness. It’s strength wrapped in gentleness. It’s leadership clothed in compassion. It’s resilience fueled by connection.

In a world where people are desperate to be seen and heard, empathy is the superpower that cuts through noise, builds bridges, and reminds us of our shared humanity.

And the best part? We all have access to it—if only we choose to use it.
